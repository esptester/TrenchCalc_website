Culvert Spacing Checks: 3 Fast Sanity Tests

Published Aug 9, 2025 · 4–6 min read · Category: Tips

Incorrect culvert spacing can lead to wasted excavation, unsafe bedding depths, and failed inspections. Here are three quick checks you can use in the field before the first bucket hits the ground.

1. Minimum Clear Distances

Most standards set minimum distances for:

  • Wall clearance — measured from culvert outside wall to trench wall.
  • Between-culvert spacing — measured outer wall to outer wall.
  • Toe clearance — space from base edge to batter or bench.

If two requirements clash, follow the stricter one — it’s cheaper than re-digging.

2. Bedding & Cover Interaction

Bedding depth supports the culvert and must remain uniform. Cover depth protects the culvert from loads above. Too little cover risks damage; too much cover means unnecessary excavation and fill.

Watch transitions near pits or changes in RL — these are common points where cover depth goes out of spec.

3. Quick Layout Workflow

  1. Mark centerlines on the surface.
  2. Apply your standard culvert spacing from the centerlines.
  3. Check wall and toe clearances.
  4. Adjust ends or middle culverts to keep within allowable limits.

Always re-run your trench volume calcs after adjusting spacing — it can change fill needs and testing zones.
